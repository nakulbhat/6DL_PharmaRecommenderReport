\begin{abstract}
Medication recommendation in outpatient environments constitutes a multi-label decision problem requiring the simultaneous consideration of therapeutic efficacy, patient-specific constraints, and drug--drug interaction safety. This paper presents three contributions: (1) a structured survey and synthesis of twenty significant studies spanning representation learning, temporal modeling, graph-based drug recommendation, and interpretability; (2) an analysis of converging findings and persistent research gaps; and (3) a proposal for a hybrid, outpatient-focused deep learning architecture incorporating integrated safety filtering and local explanations.
\end{abstract}
